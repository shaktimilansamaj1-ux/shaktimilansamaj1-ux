Knowledge & Information Hub

Whether you're a researcher, policy maker, partner organization, or community member, these resources offer valuable insights into our work and the challenges faced by vulnerable populations in Nepal.

Achievements 

Stay informed about our latest activities, achievements, and important announcements

January 15, 2025
New Partnership with UNICEF Nepal
SMS has entered into a strategic partnership with UNICEF Nepal to expand our child protection programs. This collaboration will enhance our capacity to support children affected by HIV/AIDS and trafficking, providing comprehensive care and rehabilitation services.
January 8, 2025
Annual Report 2024 Published
Our comprehensive Annual Report for 2024 is now available for download. The report highlights our achievements in serving over 1,200 beneficiaries, successful advocacy campaigns, and significant improvements in program delivery mechanisms.
December 20, 2024
World AIDS Day 2024 Campaign Success
Our World AIDS Day campaign reached over 10,000 people across 15 districts in Nepal. The campaign focused on reducing stigma and promoting testing, resulting in 500+ people getting tested for HIV and increased awareness about prevention methods.
November 25, 2024
International Day for Elimination of Violence Against Women
SMS organized awareness programs in collaboration with local governments to mark the International Day for Elimination of Violence Against Women. The events highlighted the intersection of gender-based violence and HIV vulnerability.
October 15, 2024
New Counseling Center Inaugurated
SMS inaugurated a new counseling and support center in Pokhara, expanding our reach to serve women and children in the western region of Nepal. The center provides psychosocial support, legal aid, and vocational training services.
September 30, 2024
Research Study on HIV Stigma Completed
SMS completed a comprehensive research study on HIV-related stigma in rural communities. The findings will inform policy recommendations and program improvements to better address discrimination faced by people living with HIV.
