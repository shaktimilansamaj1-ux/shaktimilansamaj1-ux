Transforming Lives: A Year of Impact in HIV Care and Support

January 15, 2025
SMS Care Team
8 min read
1,247 views

Through our comprehensive HIV care and support programs, we've provided life-changing assistance to over 500 women and children, offering not just medical treatment but hope, dignity, and a path to empowerment.

This year has been nothing short of transformational for our HIV care and support programs at Shakti Milan Samaj. As we reflect on twelve months of dedicated service, we're proud to share the profound impact we've made in the lives of women and children living with HIV/AIDS across Nepal.

Our journey began with a simple yet powerful belief: every person living with HIV deserves not just medical treatment, but comprehensive care that addresses their physical, emotional, and social needs. This holistic approach has been the cornerstone of our success, enabling us to create lasting change in communities that have long been marginalized and stigmatized.

Our Comprehensive Approach to HIV Care

At the heart of our program lies a multi-faceted approach that goes beyond traditional healthcare delivery. We understand that HIV affects not just the individual, but entire families and communities. Our comprehensive care model includes:

  • Medical Treatment and Support: Ensuring consistent access to antiretroviral therapy (ART) and regular health monitoring
  • Psychosocial Counseling: Providing emotional support and mental health services to help individuals cope with their diagnosis
  • Educational Support: Helping children affected by HIV continue their education through scholarships and school supplies
  • Income Generation Programs: Empowering women with skills training and microfinance opportunities
  • Community-Based Care: Training community health workers to provide ongoing support and reduce stigma

Community Home-Based Care: A Game Changer

Our community home-based care program has been particularly impactful, bringing healthcare directly to the doorsteps of those who need it most. This approach has not only improved health outcomes but also reduced the stigma associated with visiting healthcare facilities.

Stories of Transformation

Behind every statistic is a human story of resilience, hope, and transformation. Let us share some of the remarkable journeys we've witnessed this year.

"Shakti Milan Samaj has given me a second opportunity to be alive through its community care centre program. They provided me with so much care and medical assistance while I was struggling with physical and mental burden which is why I feel very grateful towards SMS."
- Aagya B.K., Program Beneficiary

Aagya's story is one of many that illustrate the transformative power of comprehensive care. When she first came to us, she was struggling not just with her health, but with the social isolation and stigma that often accompanies an HIV diagnosis. Through our program, she received not only medical treatment but also counseling, peer support, and skills training that helped her rebuild her life.

Breaking the Cycle of Stigma

One of our most significant achievements this year has been our work in combating HIV-related stigma. Through community education programs and peer support groups, we've helped change attitudes and create more accepting environments for people living with HIV.

Our peer educators, many of whom are women living with HIV themselves, have been instrumental in this effort. They've conducted over 100 community awareness sessions, reaching more than 3,000 community members with accurate information about HIV transmission, treatment, and the importance of supporting those affected.

Empowering Through Education

Education has been a crucial component of our empowerment strategy. This year, we've provided educational support to over 200 children affected by HIV, ensuring they can continue their studies despite the challenges their families face.

Our education support program includes:

  • School fee assistance and scholarships
  • School supplies and uniforms
  • Tutoring and academic support
  • Nutritional support to improve school attendance

Building Economic Independence

Economic empowerment has been another cornerstone of our approach. We believe that financial independence is crucial for the long-term wellbeing of women living with HIV. This year, our income generation programs have supported 150+ women through:

  • Skills training in tailoring, handicrafts, and small business management
  • Microfinance loans to start small businesses
  • Market linkage support to help women sell their products
  • Financial literacy training

Success Story: From Survivor to Entrepreneur

Mina Rai, one of our program participants, started a small tailoring business with support from our microfinance program. Today, she employs three other women and has become a role model in her community, showing that HIV doesn't define one's potential for success.

The Role of Community Health Workers

Our community health workers have been the backbone of our program's success. These dedicated individuals, many of whom are women living with HIV themselves, provide crucial support services including:

  • Home visits for medication adherence support
  • Basic health monitoring and referrals
  • Psychosocial support and counseling
  • Community education and awareness
  • Linkage to healthcare services

This year, we trained 50 new community health workers, expanding our reach into remote areas where access to healthcare services is limited.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

While we celebrate our successes, we also acknowledge the challenges we've faced and the lessons we've learned. The COVID-19 pandemic continued to impact our work, requiring us to adapt our service delivery methods and find innovative ways to maintain support for our beneficiaries.

We learned the importance of:

  • Flexible service delivery models that can adapt to changing circumstances
  • Strong community partnerships that can provide support during crises
  • Technology integration to maintain connections when physical meetings aren't possible
  • Mental health support as an integral part of HIV care

Looking Forward: Our Vision for the Future

As we look ahead, we're committed to expanding and deepening our impact. Our goals for the coming year include:

  • Reaching 750+ women and children with comprehensive care services
  • Establishing three new community care centers in underserved areas
  • Launching a youth-focused HIV prevention and education program
  • Strengthening our advocacy efforts for policy changes that protect the rights of people living with HIV
  • Developing partnerships with healthcare institutions to improve service integration

A Call to Action

The transformation we've witnessed this year is just the beginning. Every woman who regains her health, every child who continues their education, every family that finds stability – these are the building blocks of a more just and equitable society.

But we cannot do this work alone. We call upon our supporters, partners, and the broader community to join us in this mission. Whether through financial support, volunteering, or simply spreading awareness about HIV and the importance of compassionate care, everyone has a role to play in transforming lives.

Together, we can continue to break down barriers, challenge stigma, and create a world where every person living with HIV can live with dignity, hope, and the support they deserve.

"The work we do is not just about treating a medical condition – it's about restoring dignity, rebuilding lives, and creating hope where there was once despair. Every life we touch is a testament to the power of compassionate care and community support."
- SMS Care Team
